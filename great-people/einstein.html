Albert Einstein

Theoretical Physicist
Born: March 14, 1879 (Ulm, Germany) Died: April 18, 1955 (Princeton, New Jersey, USA)

Education & Early Life

Early Education in Munich

1885 - 1888: Catholic Elementary School

1888 - 1894: Luitpold Gymnasium

Swiss Federal Polytechnic School (ETH Zurich)

1896 - 1901: Teaching Diploma in Mathematics and Physics

University of Zurich

1905: PhD in Physics

Dissertation: "A New Determination of Molecular Dimensions"

Scientific Contributions

Special Theory of Relativity (1905)

Introduced the famous equation E = mcÂ² and revolutionized our understanding of space and time.

General Theory of Relativity (1915)

Provided a new understanding of gravity as a curvature of spacetime.

Photoelectric Effect

Explained how electrons are emitted from atoms when they absorb photons of light.

Bose-Einstein Condensate

Predicted a new state of matter, later confirmed in 1995.

Awards & Honors

Nobel Prize in Physics (1921)

For services to Theoretical Physics and the discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect

Copley Medal (1925)

From the Royal Society for his theory of relativity and contributions to quantum theory

Max Planck Medal (1929)

For exceptional achievements in theoretical physics

"The important thing is not to stop questioning. Curiosity has its own reason for existence."

"Peace cannot be kept by force; it can only be achieved by understanding."
