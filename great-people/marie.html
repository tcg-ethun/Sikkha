Marie Curie

Physicist & Chemist
Born: November 7, 1867 (Warsaw, Poland) Died: July 4, 1934 (Passy, France)

Education & Early Life

Early Education in Warsaw

1874 - 1883: Studied at various schools in Warsaw

1883 - 1885: Flying University (underground education)

University of Paris (Sorbonne)

1891: Physics Degree

1893: Mathematics Degree

ESPCI Paris

1894: Research on magnetic properties of steel

Met future husband Pierre Curie

Scientific Contributions

Discovery of Polonium and Radium (1898)

Isolated two new radioactive elements, naming Polonium after her native Poland.

Research on Radioactivity

Pioneered research on radioactive materials and coined the term "radioactivity".

Mobile X-Ray Units

Developed mobile radiography units for treating wounded soldiers during World War I.

Radium Institute

Founded the Radium Institute in Paris, a major center for the study of radioactivity.

Awards & Honors

Nobel Prize in Physics (1903)

Shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel for research on radiation phenomena

Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1911)

For the discovery of radium and polonium, and isolation of pure radium

Davy Medal (1903)

From the Royal Society for research on radioactive substances

Notable Quotes

"Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood. Now is the time to understand more, so that we may fear less."

"One never notices what has been done; one can only see what remains to be done."

"Be less curious about people and more curious about ideas."

"I was taught that the way of progress was neither swift nor easy."
